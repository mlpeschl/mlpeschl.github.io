Biography

Hi, my name is Markus and I am currently writing a Master’s thesis as part of the programme Applied Mathematics at the Delft University of Technology. My research deals with how we can optimize deep reinforcement learning agents for acting in accordance with a variety of human values. More generally, I am interested in combining methods of game theory, cognitive modeling, uncertainty quantification and deep learning to solve (multi-agent) reinforcement learning problems.

Regarding immediate applications of reinforcement learning, I am excited about safe and sustainable models for automation as well as advancing the progress of data-driven personalized healthcare.

Outside of mathematics, I like to spend my time playing the piano and maximizing my fitness function through calisthenics and bouldering.

ADRQN-PyTorch: A Torch implementation of the action-specific deep recurrent Q network.

Deep Q networks have proven to be an easy to implement method for solving control problems in both continuous or large discrete state spaces. The action-specific deep recurrent Q network (ADRQN) introduces an intermediate LSTM layer for remembering action-observation pairs when dealing with partial observability. This post explores a compact PyTorch implementation of the ADRQN including small scale experiments on classical control tasks.
